# Business Analytics

This repository contains teaching material, datasets, and Jupyter Notebooks for the **Business Analytics** module. The material is designed for postgraduate students who want to understand how data analytics can support business decision-making, forecasting, and strategic planning.

## Repository Overview

The repository focuses mainly on **time series analysis and forecasting** using Python. It includes practical business case studies, theoretical explanations, and applied notebooks using real-world-style datasets.

Current repository contents include:

- `Time_Series_ARX_ARMAX_Business_Analytics.ipynb`
- `time_series_case_studies_python_master.ipynb`
- `time_series_theory_master_lecture.ipynb`
- `Walmart.csv`

## Learning Objectives

By working through the notebooks in this repository, students will be able to:

- understand the fundamentals of time series analysis;
- identify trend, seasonality, cycles, and noise in business data;
- apply smoothing and forecasting methods;
- test for stationarity;
- interpret ACF and PACF plots;
- understand AR, MA, ARMA, ARIMA, ARX, and ARMAX models;
- use exogenous variables in forecasting models;
- evaluate forecasts using MAE, RMSE, and MAPE;
- interpret forecasting results in a business context.

### 3. Business Forecasting Case Study

The repository includes a business analytics case using the **Walmart sales dataset**.

The case explores how weekly sales can be forecast using both historical sales data and external business variables such as:

- holidays;
- temperature;
- fuel price;
- CPI;
- unemployment;
- seasonality indicators.

The aim is to demonstrate how forecasting can support:

- inventory planning;
- sales forecasting;
- staffing decisions;
- promotion planning;
- business decision-making.
